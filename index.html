Initiatives

Our mission is to inspire, educate, and launch the next wave of entrepreneurs from Columbia, and last year went a long way toward that goal.


Speakers

Last year, we brought great speakers, from Peter Thiel (PayPal) and Arianna Huffington (The Huffington Post) to Philippe von Borries (Refinery29) and Eric Poirier (Addepar). A few of the many, many events we held during the year were on topics like fintech, social entrepreneurship, fashion, venture capital, UI/UX, legal entrepreneurship, digital media, innovation in food, and more.


Ignite@CU

We started our first conference for undergraduate students across New York City interested in design, engineering, entrepreneurship! Companies like HUGE, Codecademy, Shapeways, Fifty-Three, and Bonobos were involved, with speakers like Jared Hecht (founder of GroupMe and Fundera) and Scott Kurnit (founder of About.com and Keep).


#StartupColumbia and CVC

We went into a second year with #StartupColumbia, our entrepreneurship festival organized in collaboration with Columbia Entrepreneurship and Columbia Entrepreneurs Organization! Panels in the festival revolved around topics like fashion innovation, fintech, adtech, and more. CVC, or the Columbia Venture Competition, was bigger than before, with a $250,000 pool for student startups to compete in five challenges.


Tech Treks

We launched our first round of global tech treks with two groups of great students, heading to London and Paris to visit startups across the Atlantic! Closer to home, we also visited companies in New York City, with tech treks to places like Google and Foursquare.


Women@CORE

The program to educate and mentor more women interested in startups was in its second year. Mentors included founders from startups like Sarah Adler (Spoon University) and Tze Chun (Uprise Art) to engineers at Google and managers at Etsy, and we also continued our speaker series of women in tech.


Startup Expo

We brought startups big and small to Low Steps on campus for a day to demo and reach students on campus. Some of the companies involved were Lyft, Cuff, Harry's, Venture For America, and Fiverr.
